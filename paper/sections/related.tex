\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

\subsection{RL amplifies what exists: the missing metacognitive substrate}
\label{sec:related:rlvr}

The dominant recipe for reasoning post-training is reinforcement learning
from verifiable rewards (RLVR) driven by a single terminal correctness
signal. \citet{shao2024deepseekmath} introduced GRPO, the critic-free
group-relative policy-gradient method that made outcome-reward RL practical
at scale for mathematical reasoning; DeepSeek-R1 \citep{deepseekr1} showed
that applying this recipe to a strong base model elicits long chains of
thought in which reflective-looking behavior---re-checking,
``aha''-style backtracking---\emph{emerges} without ever being rewarded as
such; SimpleRL-Zoo \citep{zeng2025simplerlzoo} replicated zero-RL across a
zoo of open base models and found that both the gains and the emergent
reflective behavior vary sharply with the base model family; and the Qwen3
report \citep{qwen3} folds the same pipeline into production post-training.
Across this entire line the objective contains one bit: was the final answer
correct. Credit is smeared over the whole trajectory, so a verification step
that actually flipped the outcome is indistinguishable, from the optimizer's
point of view, from decorative hedging that happened to co-occur with a
correct answer. Process supervision \citep{lightman2024letsverify} sharpens
credit to individual steps and outperforms outcome supervision on MATH, but
it scores steps against \emph{externally labeled} step correctness; it never
asks whether a step changed the \emph{model's own} evidence about the
answer, and so it cannot separate a step that is merely correct from one
that is epistemically useful.

\citet{gandhi2025cognitive} sharpen why this matters. They show that four
cognitive behaviors---verification, backtracking, subgoal setting, and
backward chaining---predict which base models benefit from RL, and that RL
\emph{amplifies behaviors already present} in the base model or its SFT
prime: priming with the behaviors enables gains even when the primed
solutions contain wrong answers. This establishes that the substrate must be
supplied before RL can grow it, but it stops at the level of surface form.
Priming installs the \emph{shape} of a habit, and amplification is
indiscriminate---nothing in the pipeline checks that an amplified
verification actually verifies. Our design accepts the first half (we prime
explicit \metaopen{}\dots\metaclose{} blocks via SFT) and targets the
gap the second half leaves open: a reward that pays a primed metacognitive
block only when it does epistemic work, so amplification becomes selective
rather than indiscriminate (Section~\ref{sec:method}).

\subsection{Self-distillation degrades reasoning: rewarding the wrong tokens}
\label{sec:related:selfdistill}

Turning a model's own likelihoods into a training signal is attractive
because it needs no external teacher, but a fast-growing line shows the
signal is treacherous: it is strong, yet it rewards the wrong tokens unless
carefully aimed. \citet{kim2026selfdistill} demonstrate the headline
failure---naive self-distillation on top of RLVR can \emph{degrade}
reasoning relative to plain GRPO---so ``the model teaching itself'' is not
automatically benign. Their RLRT variant \citep{kim2026rebellious} then shows
the flip side: a frozen reference model's likelihoods carry genuinely useful
training information when the sign is \emph{reversed} (the ``Rebellious
Student'' ascends rather than descends the teacher's signal), improving over
GRPO on MATH500 and AIME24. \citet{whydegrade2026} localize the mechanism:
conditioning distillation on the gold solution systematically suppresses
\emph{deliberation} tokens---the hedging and re-derivation that carry
multi-step search---because those tokens look low-probability once the answer
is known, and the loss therefore shortens the very reasoning paths that hard
problems need. A cluster of on-policy-distillation work catalogs the same
pathology and proposes recoveries: \citet{opd2026rethinking} characterize the
failure modes (distribution gap, homogeneous-teacher overfitting) and
prescribe off-policy initialization plus teacher-aligned prompt selection;
\citet{sdzero2026} replace binary rewards with a generator--reviser scheme
that manufactures token-level dense supervision without an external teacher;
\citet{opsd2025} formalize a single model playing student and teacher at
once; \citet{gates2025} and \citet{hdpo2025} add consensus gating and
privileged self-distillation to blunt the side effects; and
\citet{lightning_opd2026} precompute teacher log-probabilities to reproduce
OPD without a live teacher server. \citet{mulgund2026rlsd} unify the family,
distilling a self-teacher's gold-conditioned distribution jointly with RLVR.

The work closest to ours is AntiSD \citep{shen2026antisd}, which supplies the
sharpest diagnosis of \emph{which} tokens self-distillation misprices. It
defines a per-token conditional pointwise mutual information,
$u_t = \log\big[\pi(y_t\mid x, c, y_{<t}) / \pi(y_t\mid x, y_{<t})\big]$ with
$c$ a verified solution, and shows that privileged context \emph{inflates}
the teacher on tokens already implied by the answer (boilerplate, structural
scaffolding) and \emph{deflates} it on the deliberation tokens that drive
search. AntiSD's remedy is to \emph{ascend} the student--teacher divergence,
reversing the per-token sign for a bounded one-step advantage, and it reaches
GRPO accuracy in far fewer steps with up to $+11.5$ points on competition
math. We share AntiSD's premise---gold-conditioned self-imitation
mechanically rewards shortcut and boilerplate tokens---but occupy a different
corner. AntiSD acts as a global, per-token imitation objective with a
reversed sign over the whole trajectory; we reward a \emph{shift} (a
difference across a span) of a gold-\emph{versus-decoy} discriminative margin,
localized to an explicit \metaopen{}\dots\metaclose{} span, and validate the
installed substrate against a matched-base causal twin rather than an
accuracy curve alone (Section~\ref{sec:method}). Crucially the shortcut risk
AntiSD identifies is \emph{shared}: our verify-boilerplate is their
scaffolding bias, and a span-shift reward could in principle be collected by
emitting a formulaic ``let me check'' that moves no evidence. This is exactly
why our decomposition (an imitation-style Gandhi arm and a shift-only arm) and
placebo tests are load-bearing rather than optional (Section~\ref{sec:method})
---they are what separate a rewarded metacognitive \emph{act} from rewarded
metacognitive \emph{decoration}.

\subsection{The field converged on reference-answer-likelihood rewards}
\label{sec:related:likelihood}

A parallel line, arriving from the verifier-free direction, has converged on
the same underlying quantity we exploit: the reference answer's likelihood
under the model. RLPR \citep{yu2025rlpr} makes this explicit---it replaces the
external verifier with the model's own average decoding probability of the
gold answer tokens, arguing that intrinsic probability \emph{is} the model's
self-evaluation of its reasoning, and extends RLVR to general domains on that
signal. CRM \citep{zhang2025crm} reaches the answer likelihood from the
process side: it models each step's reward as the conditional probability of
staying on a correct-outcome trajectory, so that the summed process reward is
the log-probability of the correct answer, and shows this links intermediate
credit to the final outcome without ground-truth verification. Contrastive
and self-rewarding methods circle the same object from the preference angle:
DistiLLM-2 \citep{ko2024distillm2} and RLCD \citep{yang2024rlcd} exploit
paired positive/negative responses, while Self-Rewarding models
\citep{yuan2024selfrewarding}, Meta-Rewarding \citep{wu2024metarewarding},
SPPO \citep{wu2024sppo}, and Iterative Reasoning Preference Optimization
\citep{xu2024iterativedpo} iterate a policy against its own judgments; and an
information-theoretic analysis of PPO reasoning habits \citep{fourhabits2026wn}
studies how these signals reshape behavior. What none of these localize is
\emph{where} in the trajectory the likelihood moves and \emph{against what
alternative}. RLPR and CRM score the gold answer's probability globally or
step-averaged; the preference methods reward whole responses. We sit
squarely in this reference-answer-likelihood neighborhood, and we are honest
that our novelty is correspondingly narrow: we do not introduce a new family
of signal but a specific instantiation---a gold-versus-\emph{decoy} contrast
(not the gold likelihood alone), measured as a \emph{shift} across an explicit
metacognition span (not globally or per step), so that credit reaches the
metacognitive act that moved the model's evidence rather than the answer
tokens that merely record it.

\subsection{Calibration is not control: from knowing uncertainty to using it}
\label{sec:related:calibration}

Our motivation is a gap this convergence leaves untouched: a model can be
made to \emph{report} its uncertainty accurately without ever being made to
\emph{act} on it. A long line establishes the first half.
\citet{guo2017calibration} made miscalibration measurable (expected
calibration error, which we adopt for RQ4); \citet{kadavath2022language}
showed large LMs are mostly well-calibrated when probed after generation
about whether their own answers are true (P(True), P(IK)); and verbalized-
confidence work \citep{lin2022teaching, tian2023just} trains or prompts models
to state calibrated uncertainty in words. In all of these calibration is the
\emph{terminal} object: the metric rewards accurately reporting doubt, not
resolving it, and a perfectly calibrated model can correctly doubt every hard
problem and still fail all of them. Rewarding Doubt
\citep{baniharouni2026rewardingdoubtreinforcementlearning} pushes calibration
into RL by directly rewarding calibrated confidence expressions; we reuse a
Rewarding-Doubt-style log-scoring term, but treat calibration as an
intermediate signal for control rather than the goal---whether overconfidence
drops on hard wrong answers, and whether that drop is \emph{used} to redirect
the solution, are separate questions.

The converse line---can models \emph{use} self-assessment---is cautionary.
Self-Refine \citep{madaan2023selfrefine} improves outputs by iterative
self-critique, but \citet{huang2024llmcannot} show that on reasoning, without
external feedback, intrinsic self-correction fails to improve and can degrade
accuracy: prompting alone does not convert latent self-knowledge into better
answers. Training-based approaches go further but stop short of the
in-reasoning act. STaR \citep{zelikman2022star} fine-tunes on self-generated
rationales filtered by answer correctness, reinforcing whole successful traces
without crediting any epistemic act inside them; SCoRe \citep{kumar2024score}
trains multi-turn self-correction with RL, but its reward attaches to the
outcome of a \emph{revised} attempt, so credit lives at episode granularity,
between complete solutions, not at the mid-solution moment where the model's
evidence goes wrong; and Reflexion \citep{shinn2023reflexionlanguageagentsverbal}
shows verbal reflection can fix a \emph{next} trial in an external agent loop.
Our problem is narrower and internal: within a single attempt, when
confidence wavers, does that wavering cause an explicit diagnosis and a course
correction, and does the correction pay off in accuracy.

These three lines triangulate our contribution. Outcome-only RLVR cannot
select for functional metacognition because its credit never reaches the
metacognitive act; self-distillation supplies a strong but mis-aimed signal
that rewards boilerplate over deliberation; the field's reference-answer-
likelihood rewards score that signal globally, never localized to a
metacognitive span or contrasted against a decoy; and the calibration
literature optimizes reporting doubt rather than using it. \pmishift{}
occupies the remaining corner: for each \metaopen{}\dots\metaclose{} block we
measure the model's gold-versus-decoy log-likelihood contrast immediately
before and immediately after the block under a frozen reference, and route the
resulting \emph{shift} onto the block's tokens and nothing else. Because
multiple reward terms can collide, the RL stage normalizes each reward
independently (GDPO-style decoupled normalization,
\citealp{liu2026gdpogrouprewarddecouplednormalization}), and a matched-base
twin---identical meta-stripped data, byte-audited identical non-meta
hyperparameters, correctness-only GRPO---measures whether the installed
substrate causally pays rather than merely survives
(Section~\ref{sec:method}).
