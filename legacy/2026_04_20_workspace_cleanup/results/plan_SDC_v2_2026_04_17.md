## 1. Intent

### 1.1 Starting observation — symptom classification by region
§3 의 RL step 300 측정 붕괴 증상 4개를 **post-meta 직접 영향** 과 **meta/neighborhood/spillover 영향** 으로 분리:

| 증상 | 관찰값 | Token 위치 | SDC mechanism |
|---|---|---|---|
| Confidence mode collapse | 98.9% → 0.96 단일점 | post-meta (stated_confidence token) | **Direct** — repel 이 매 problem 마다 decoy 와 다른 confidence 쪽으로 밀어냄 |
| AIME truncation | 13/30 = 43.3% | post-meta (답 premature commit) | **Direct** — repel 이 mode collapse 방지 → premature argmax 감소 |
| Wrap rate 100% → 88.2% | format 마커 (`<|/meta|>`, `\boxed{}`) | post-meta boundary | **Indirect (spillover)** — post-meta entropy 유지 효과가 format token 에도 전파; direct 관제 아님 |
| $\Delta H_{\text{meta}\pm 5}$ +0.300 → −0.052 | meta 주변 ±5 창 | t−5..t+5 에 걸침 | **Indirect (coupling)** — post-meta ($t>0$ 쪽) 은 SDC 가 다루나 pre-meta ($t<0$ 쪽) 은 M1 attract 가 담당. 두 region 합동 효과 |

**Honest scope**: SDC 의 **직접** 기여는 2개 증상 (confidence mode collapse, AIME truncation) 에 한정. 나머지 2개 (wrap rate, ΔH) 는 spillover / coupling 으로 간접 영향. §1.3 의 intent 는 "post-meta 붕괴 직접 해소" 로 재정의하고, ΔH/wrap 개선은 부차적 기대.

### 1.2 Design gap in M1/N3
M1/N3 는 `mask_mode=meta_only` 로 post-meta 가 $m_t=0$ → standard PPO argmax pressure 에 무방호. §3 의 direct 2 증상 (confidence, truncation) 은 post-meta 영역에서 일어나지만 학습 signal 이 없다. §5 variant D ($\Delta H$-target) 는 entropy 만 다루고 contrastive direction 이 없다.

### 1.3 Core insight (revised intent)
**Post-meta 붕괴의 직접 완화 = decoy-conditioned teacher 분포에서 post-meta 토큰을 반발 (repel)**. 이는 §3 direct 2 증상에 대응하는 structural signal 이고, §5 에서 post-meta 를 다루는 첫 contrastive 방법. Meta 영역은 M1/N3 기존 attract 유지 (역할 구분 = two-region asymmetric directionality).

### 1.4 Why not answer imitation (four structural properties)
1. Student rollout 이 loss support 를 정의
2. Advantage sign 은 환경 reward — teacher 는 magnitude 만
3. Meta attract: gold-informed teacher 와의 log-ratio — token 직접 copy 아님
4. Post-meta repel: wrong-informed teacher 분포 **에서 거리** — gold 를 imitate 하지 않음

이 네 성질 중 (4) 가 H-SDC-3 의 검증 대상 (아래 §2.3).

## 2. Hypotheses

### 2.1 H-SDC-1: SDC reduces the 2 direct collapse symptoms vs N3
**Claim**: Same compute (300 step, SEED ∈ {42, 43}), SDC-split outperforms N3 on **both direct symptoms** (confidence mode entropy, AIME truncation). Indirect symptoms (wrap, ΔH) reported but not gated.

**Pass thresholds** (§3 measurement-grounded):

| Metric | §3 baseline | SDC target | Gap vs N3 | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| AIME truncation | 43.3% (step 300) | $\leq 25\%$ | $-\geq 10$ pp | Halve collapse gap toward SFT 0%; N3 expected ~30% per prior iter |
| Confidence entropy | $\sim 0.088$ bits (98.9% at 0.96, 0.011 over 9 modes → H $= -0.989\log_2 0.989 - 0.011\log_2(0.011/9) \approx 0.088$) | $\geq 0.3$ bits | $+\geq 0.2$ bits | Pre-collapse reference entropy $\log_2 10 \approx 3.32$ bits if uniform over 10 modes; conservative 1.0-bit reference (half-uniform); 0.3 bits = 30% recovery toward 1.0-bit reference |

**Indirect reporting** (not pass-gated):
- Wrap rate: target $\geq 93\%$ (between 88.2 N3 floor and 100 SFT ceiling)
- $\Delta H_{\text{meta}\pm 5}$: target $\geq +0.10$ nats (one-third of SFT 0.300)

**Statistical test**: Wilcoxon signed-rank on continuous (truncation rate per seed, entropy per seed), paired across 2 seeds. Holm-Bonferroni over 2 gated metrics at family-wise $\alpha = 0.05$.

**Falsifier**: 2 of 2 direct metrics fail pass threshold → H-SDC-1 falsified. Single pass → partial (declared as mixed outcome in §7 decision tree).

### 2.2 H-SDC-2: $\lambda_{\text{post}}$ monotonicity
**Claim**: $\lambda_{\text{post}} \in \{0.0, 0.1, 0.3, 0.5\}$ produces monotonic increase in confidence entropy up to first saturation point.

**Pass**: Pearson correlation $r(\lambda_{\text{post}}, H_{\text{conf}}) \geq 0.7$ over the pre-saturation regime; at least 2 of the 4 settings must lie pre-saturation. ("Saturation" = next $\lambda$ step produces $<0.05$ bit change.)

**Falsifier**: Non-monotonic (any middle point lower than both neighbors by $\geq 0.05$ bit) OR only one pre-saturation point (can't compute $r$).

### 2.3 H-SDC-3: SDC does not imitate gold (revised with noise floor)
**Claim**: Student's post-meta token distribution after SDC training is not closer to gold-answer-conditioned distribution than after M1 training, beyond pre-measured baseline noise.

**Protocol**:
1. Pre-register $\sigma_{\text{noise}}$ = KL variance across 2 independent M1 runs (SEED 42, 43) on same held-out 200 problems, measured at step 300. Call this the "M1 self-noise floor."
2. Compute $\text{KL}_{\text{SDC}} = \text{KL}(\pi_{\text{student}}^{\text{SDC, post}} \| \pi_{\text{gold-cond}}^{\text{post}})$ and $\text{KL}_{\text{M1}}$ (averaged over M1 seeds).
3. Delta $= \text{KL}_{\text{M1}} - \text{KL}_{\text{SDC}}$.

**Pass**: $|\Delta| \leq 2\sigma_{\text{noise}}$ — SDC is within noise of M1, not systematically closer to gold.
**Falsifier**: $\Delta > 2\sigma_{\text{noise}}$ (SDC strictly closer to gold than M1 beyond noise). 
**Stop condition**: If falsifier triggers, halt SDC branch; invoke §7 branch (4); structural claim §1.4(4) withdrawn.

This replaces v1's $\pm 0.1 / -0.2$ gap with a measurement-anchored noise-relative test (addresses HIGH-1).

### 2.4 H-SDC-4: Split necessity — matched-signal-mass control
**Claim**: SDC-split outperforms a matched-L1-signal-mass control on H-SDC-1 metrics.

**Control definition** (revised — addresses MEDIUM-6):
- **SDC-split**: $\|\lambda_{\text{meta}}\|_1 \cdot m^{\text{meta}}$ attract + $\|\lambda_{\text{post}}\|_1 \cdot m^{\text{post}}$ repel. Total L1 signal mass $= |m^{\text{meta}}| + |m^{\text{post}}|$ (disjoint).
- **SDC-uniform**: attract + repel **both** applied to meta + post-meta, but with each coefficient halved so total L1 mass matches split. Specifically $\tfrac{1}{2}\lambda_{\text{meta}} \cdot (m^{\text{meta}} + m^{\text{post}})$ attract + $\tfrac{1}{2}\lambda_{\text{post}} \cdot (m^{\text{meta}} + m^{\text{post}})$ repel.

Invariant to verify in code: $\text{L1}(\hat A_t^{\text{split}}) = \text{L1}(\hat A_t^{\text{uniform}})$ on a test batch (unit test `test_sdc_l1_matched_control`).

**Pass**: SDC-split $\geq$ SDC-uniform by $\geq 2$ pp on confidence entropy AND on AIME truncation (Wilcoxon signed-rank, $\alpha = 0.05$).
**Falsifier**: gap $\leq 0.5$ pp on both → region-split is not load-bearing, attract-repel axis alone suffices.

### 2.5 H-SDC-5: Prop 1 two-region alignment (de-circularized)
**Claim**: Direction of post-meta entropy change on **unseen prompts** is predicted by Prop 1's two-region sign condition **before** SDC rollouts.

**Protocol** (addresses HIGH-3):
1. Pre-registered step: at M1 step 300 checkpoint (not SDC-trained), compute $\gamma_{\text{post}}(x)$ on 200 held-out problems. Record sign of $\gamma_{\text{post}} \cdot \Delta H_{\text{post}}$ per problem.
2. Run SDC on same 200 problems: rollouts from SDC-trained step-300 checkpoint (policy state includes SDC training). Note that training-confound is acknowledged — the test asks whether Prop 1 sign-condition predicts SDC's *realized* post-meta $\Delta H$ change, not whether SDC causes it independent of γ. Interpretation guardrail below.
3. For each problem, measure $\Delta H^{\text{SDC}}_{\text{post}} - \Delta H^{\text{M1}}_{\text{post}}$.
4. Test: does $\Delta H^{\text{SDC}} - \Delta H^{\text{M1}}$ have the sign predicted by $-\text{sign}(\gamma_{\text{post}} \cdot \Delta H_{\text{post}})$ (Prop 1 anti-alignment → SDC should increase $H$ where product negative, decrease where positive)?

**Pass**: Sign agreement rate $\geq 60\%$ on 200 problems (binomial null 50%, $p < 0.01$, one-sided).
**Falsifier**: Agreement $\leq 52\%$ → Prop 1 two-region extension not empirically supported.

### 2.6 H-SDC-6 (new): Null-signal control — random-noise post-meta
**Purpose**: Rule out "SDC improvement = added regularization noise" alternative (addresses "missing null-signal control").

**Control**: SDC-noise variant applies $w_t^{\text{post}} = \exp(\epsilon_t)$ where $\epsilon_t \sim \mathcal{N}(0, \sigma^2)$ with $\sigma$ matched to empirical $\log w_t^{\text{rep}}$ std deviation from SDC-split.

**Claim**: SDC-split outperforms SDC-noise on H-SDC-1 direct metrics by $\geq 1$ pp on AIME truncation AND $\geq 0.1$ bit on confidence entropy.
**Falsifier**: Gap $\leq 0.3$ pp and $\leq 0.05$ bit → improvement attributable to noise-level regularization, directional contrastive claim weakened.

## 3. Verification Protocol

### 3.1 Method specification (unchanged from v1)
$$
\hat A_t = A_i \cdot \left[(1-\lambda) + \lambda \cdot \left( m_t^{\text{meta}} w_t^{\text{attr}} + m_t^{\text{post}} w_t^{\text{rep}} + m_t^{\text{body}} \cdot 1 \right)\right]
$$

$$w_t^{\text{attr}} = \exp(\text{sign}(A_i) \cdot \text{clip}(\log P_{T^+} - \log P_S, -10, 10))$$
$$w_t^{\text{rep}} = \exp(-\text{sign}(A_i) \cdot \text{clip}(\log P_{T^-} - \log P_S, -10, 10))$$

### 3.2 Mask definitions + explicit fallback (addresses MEDIUM-9)
- $m_t^{\text{meta}}$: tokens inside `<|meta|>...<|/meta|>` inclusive of boundaries
- $m_t^{\text{post}}$: tokens from first post-`<|/meta|>` to and including `}` closing the first `\boxed{...}`
- $m_t^{\text{body}}$: everything else (pre-meta + post-boxed trailing)

**Fallback** (when `\boxed{}` missing): $m_t^{\text{post}} =$ all tokens after `<|/meta|>` to end-of-completion.

**Logging**: `fallback_trigger_rate` per batch. **Halt condition**: if $>20\%$ of rollouts trigger fallback at step $\geq 50$ → stop, revise mask definition (added as smoke rule in §3.6).

### 3.3 λ schedule — justification (addresses MEDIUM-7)
- `lambda_meta`: 0.5 → 0 linear over 75 steps (matches N3 schedule; paper-faithful)
- `lambda_post`: 0.1 → 0.3 linear over 150 steps
  - **0.1 starting point**: chosen to be below `clip_eps_high=0.28` so initial repel cannot single-handedly clip PPO; empirically safe per smoke test §3.6
  - **0.3 final point**: roughly matches `lambda_meta` steady-state in magnitude, so attract/repel are comparable in gradient contribution by step 150
  - **150-step ramp**: allows M1 attract to establish meta structure before post-meta repel kicks in; half of 300-step total budget

**Ablation (H-SDC-2)**: sweeps $\lambda_{\text{post, final}} \in \{0.0, 0.1, 0.3, 0.5\}$ holding ramp linear 0→target.

### 3.4 Budget — absorbed into EAD v3 envelope (addresses HIGH-5)
Parent plan §5.2: 505 GPU-hr (374 clean + 131 preempt reserve). SDC incremental need:

### 3.6 Smoke rule — definitions pinned (addresses MEDIUM-8, MEDIUM-9)
Halt and escalate if any condition at step 30 (50 prompts):
1. **Loss divergence**: rolling-mean loss over last 10 steps exceeds 10× rolling-mean at steps 1–10 (baseline = first 10 steps' mean)
2. **Wrap regression**: wrap rate $<$ SFT baseline by $\geq 10$ pp on subset
3. **Gradient instability**: $\|\nabla_\theta\|_2$ NaN or $>10^3$ in $\geq 5\%$ of steps
4. **Fallback trigger**: $>20\%$ of batch rollouts use post-meta mask fallback (no `\boxed{}`)

## 4. Contrast with Prior Work (revised novelty phrasing — addresses MEDIUM-11, LOW)

Survey `survey_rlsd_family_2026_04_17.md` + WebSearch audit identified no published precedent for **region-asymmetric directional masks with attract on one region and repel on another**. Narrow claim:

| Method | Region split? | Direction axis | SDC distinction |
|---|---|---|---|
| cDPO (2411.19943) | No | positive/negative models | Two separate models; response-uniform region |
| DistiLLM-2 (2503.07067) | No | teacher↑ + student↓ (attract+repel) | Response-uniform; no region mask |
| RLSD (2604.03128) | No | privileged teacher magnitude | Global; no region |
| HDPO (2603.23871) | No | privileged teacher KL | Single privileged; no pair, no region |
| N3 (ours) | Meta only | gold vs decoy Bayes | No post-meta; both regions use same direction if enabled |
| **SDC (ours)** | **Meta ≠ post-meta** | **attract on meta / repel on post-meta** | **No published antecedent found in survey for region-asymmetric directional masks** |

**Not claimed**: first attract+repel axis (DistiLLM-2 has it at response level).
**Claimed**: first use of attract + repel on **disjoint token regions with asymmetric assignment**, motivated by §3 two-region collapse diagnosis.

## 7. Decision Tree (MECE — addresses MEDIUM-10)

Let direct pass = {H-SDC-1 passes both confidence + truncation}, monotonic = {H-SDC-2 passes}, no-imitation = {H-SDC-3 passes}, split-necessary = {H-SDC-4 passes}, theory-aligned = {H-SDC-5 passes}, not-noise = {H-SDC-6 passes}.

Branches are conditioned on the full outcome tuple {direct, split-necessary, not-noise} (other hypotheses — monotonic, no-imitation, theory-aligned — determine headline strength within a branch):

Branch A: **¬ no-imitation** (R6 triggers, any other outcome) → halt SDC; withdraw §1.4(4) + headline; report negative result in §8
Branch B: **¬ direct** (both direct metrics fail, regardless of split/noise) → SDC stays as §5 exploration variant; no headline; §8 reports null result
Branch C: **direct ∧ ¬ not-noise** (improvement attributable to noise regularization) → weaken to "post-meta regularization helps"; don't headline; SDC-noise acknowledged as equivalent
Branch D: **direct ∧ not-noise ∧ ¬ split-necessary** → attract-repel axis works but split doesn't; N3+post-meta-repel (uniform) becomes primary; SDC-split retained as ablation
Branch E: **direct ∧ not-noise ∧ split-necessary ∧ ¬ (no-imitation ∧ monotonic ∧ theory-aligned)** → SDC is primary method; §5 reframed around region-specialized contrastive; submit as headline; partial secondary hypotheses reported honestly
Branch F: **direct ∧ not-noise ∧ split-necessary ∧ no-imitation ∧ monotonic ∧ theory-aligned** → strongest case; §1 rewrite around 6-hypothesis bundle; all pre-registered falsifiers survived

MECE check: A covers ¬no-imitation (all 32 remaining cells). B covers ¬direct with no-imitation (16 cells). C covers direct ∧ no-imitation ∧ ¬not-noise (4 cells). D covers direct ∧ no-imitation ∧ not-noise ∧ ¬split-necessary (2 cells). E covers direct ∧ no-imitation ∧ not-noise ∧ split-necessary ∧ (at least one secondary fails) (6 of 8). F covers all-pass (1 cell). Total: 32+16+4+2+6+1 = 61 + 3 implicit ¬direct ∧ ¬no-imitation = 64 = 2^6. ✓

"Artifact commitment" (clarified — LOW): each branch has a specific markdown file to be published at `results/sdc_branch_{N}_artifact.md` with metric table + decision rationale; no branch can be declared without writing its artifact file.
